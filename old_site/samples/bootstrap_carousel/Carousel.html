My IBM Career

IBM East Fishkill

The former IBM East Fishkill facility hosted a 300mm chip fabrication plant and offices for employees in the Microelectronics division, which was sold to GlobalFoundries in 2015. I worked in the Engineering & Technology Services group there from 2004-2006.

Advantest 93000 Test System

This is an Advantest chip test system. I assisted in developing a test program for a system like it to verify chips on a wafer. This line of test systems has been produced by several companies, staring with HP, then Agilent & Verigy, and now Advantest.

Circuit Simulation

This is the output of a simple circuit simulation. I worked on 2 circuit simulation projects for IBM. One was extracting specs from a circuit library to be used in simulations. The other was developing a logic simulator to mimic the circuit design & verify the output was as expected.

Xbox 360 Processor

This is the processor for the Xbox 360 game console, which was jointly developed by IBM & Microsoft. It is based on the IBM PowerPC architecture. I was part of a team developing test cases for the design & gathering data for failed cases to pass along to the developers.

IBM Poughkeepsie

The IBM Poughkeepsie site houses manufacturing & development for p & z series servers. I worked in software development & technical support there from 1997-2004.

IBM Z Mainframe

The IBM Z Mainframe is the newest version of mainframe computers dating back to the System/360. These mainframes are designed for zero downtime and high performance. I worked in development & technical support for operating system software for these mainframes.

z/OS

z/OS is the current incarnation of the mainfame operating system that was previously called MVS. I developed & provided technical support for components of this operating system.

Language Environment

Language Environment is the component of z/OS that provides a runtime environment for programs written in C, C++, COBOL, Fortran, & PL/I. I worked in development & technical support for this component.

zFS

zFS is the high performance UNIX file system for z/OS, developed to replace the existing HFS file system. I worked as part of the team that was updating the zFS prototype with the functionality necessary to be deliverable.
